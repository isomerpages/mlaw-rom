<h2>Information for Licensed Moneylenders</h2>
<p><strong>1. Where can I find more information on the Registry of Moneylenders ("the Registry")?</strong>
<br>You can find more information on the Registry of Moneylenders' <a href="https://rom.mlaw.gov.sg/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">website</a>.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>2. Where can I get a copy of the Moneylenders Act 2008 and its Rules?</strong>
<br>You may refer to our <a href="https://rom.mlaw.gov.sg/information-for-moneylenders/relevant-laws-and-regulations/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">website</a> for
the links to all the relevant laws and regulations of moneylending.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>3. Will I need a moneylender's licence for my business?</strong>
<br>Under section 3 of the Moneylenders Act 2008, any person other than an
excluded moneylender who lends a sum of money in consideration of a larger
sum being repaid shall be presumed, until the contrary is proved, to be
a moneylender. If you are unsure, you should obtain legal advice from your
solicitor as to whether your intended business activity amounts to moneylending
for which a licence is required.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>4. How do I apply for a moneylender's licence?</strong>
<br>The Registry has temporarily suspended the grant of any new moneylending
licences.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>5. What is the validity period of a moneylender's licence?</strong>
<br>The moneylender's licence is valid for a period of 12 months.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>6. How much is the application fee for a moneylender's licence?</strong>
<br>The application fee for a moneylending licence is $600 and is non-refundable.
It applies only to applications for a new licence, but not to renewals
of existing licences.</p>
<p>Please note that there is currently a suspension on the processing of
new applications for a moneylender's licence.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>7. How much is the moneylender's licence fee?</strong>
<br>The licence fee is $1,500 per annum.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>8. How do I apply for additional place(s) of business?</strong>
<br>There is a concurrent suspension on the processing of applications for
additional place(s) of business.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>9. When do I need to secure a place of business for my moneylending business?</strong>
<br>You only need to indicate your proposed place of business when applying
for a moneylender's licence. If the Registry finds you suitable to be issued
with a moneylender's licence, you will be required to produce documentary
evidence of having secured the use of suitable premises for the purpose
of your moneylending business, before the licence is issued to you.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>10. How do I register for the Moneylender’s Test? How much does the Moneylender’s Test cost?</strong>
<br>You may click <a href="https://www.gobusiness.gov.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">here</a> to
access the Moneylender’s Test Booking System to register for a test slot.
A non-refundable test fee of S$130 (inclusive of GST) is payable per attempt.</p>
<p>The Moneylender’s Test is an open book format which includes 30 multiple
choice questions which will be based on the content from the <a href="https://rom.mlaw.gov.sg/information-for-moneylenders/relevant-laws-and-regulations/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">relevant laws and regulations</a>,
including but not limited to the Moneylenders Act 2008, Moneylenders Rules
2009 and Moneylenders (Prevention of Money Laundering, Terrorism Financing
and Proliferation Financing) Rules 2009.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>11. What are the criteria to register for the Moneylender’s Test?</strong>
<br>Anyone may register for the Moneylender’s Test. However, do note that
passing the Moneylender’s Test does not automatically qualify a person
as a Test-Qualified Manager (please refer to question 6).
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>12. When and how would I know if I have passed the Moneylender’s Test?</strong>
<br>You may check your test results via <a href="https://www.gobusiness.gov.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">GoBusiness</a> after one week from
the date of your test.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>13. How do I get appointed as a Test-Qualified Manager of an approved place of business of the licensee?</strong>
<br>To be appointed as a Test-Qualified Manager, the person must satisfy the
following criteria:</p>
<p>(a) Passed the Moneylenders’ Test;</p>
<p>(b) Minimum four GCE ‘O’ Levels passes including English Language, or
equivalent; and</p>
<p>(c) Fit and proper, and of good character</p>
<p>Please note that the licensee must submit an application to the Registry
via <a href="https://www.gobusiness.gov.sg/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">GoBusiness</a> to
appoint the person as a Test-Qualified Manager.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>14. What should licensed moneylenders do if they wish to appoint a new director, manager, partner or substantial shareholder?</strong>
<br>All licensed moneylenders must seek the prior approval of the Registrar
of Moneylenders before appointing any new director, managing director,
manager, partner or substantial shareholder. Please visit our <a href="https://eservices.mlaw.gov.sg/rom/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">website</a> for
the respective links on the application.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>15. How do I update the particulars of my moneylending business?</strong>
<br>Please <a href="https://www.gobusiness.gov.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">submit</a> an
online application for the following updates:</p>
<p>(a) Change of business address (please refer to 7.1 and 7.2);</p>
<p>(b) Change of business name;</p>
<p>(c) Change of business contact details;</p>
<p>(d) Appointment of a director or any individual who takes part in the
management of the moneylending business;</p>
<p>(e) Employment or engagement of any individual for the moneylending business;</p>
<p>(f) Cessation of employment of any individual mentioned in (d) and (e);
and</p>
<p>(g) The allotment of new shares/transfer of shares (please refer to 7.3)</p>
<p>7.1 For update (a), the licensee will be required to obtain a copy of
the letter of approval from the landlord of the proposed premises for a
moneylending business to be conducted and photographs of the proposed place
of business. The letter of approval must be forwarded to the Registry within
seven days of the online application, failing which the progress and outcome
of which can be adversely affected. Please note that a tenancy agreement
is not required to be submitted to the Registry at this juncture.</p>
<p>7.2 After the Registrar has deemed the proposed premises to be suitable
for moneylending business to be conducted, the licensee will then be required
to forward to the Registry the following documents:</p>
<p>(i) A copy of the tenancy agreement of the premises covering the entire/
remaining licence period;</p>
<p>(ii) A copy of the proof of ownership of the premises in the form of property
tax or strata title deed; and</p>
<p>(iii) Proposed photo(s) of the signage to be displayed conspicuously at
the business premises.</p>
<p>7.3 For update (g), please <a href="https://www.gobusiness.gov.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">submit</a> an online application with
the following information:</p>
<p>(i) A clear copy of an up-to-date identification card of the transferor
and the transferee (if applicable);</p>
<p>(ii) Number of shares to be transferred/allotted;</p>
<p>(iii) Reason(s) for the change of shares;</p>
<p>(iv) The agreed price for transferring the shares;</p>
<p>(v) Documentary evidence of the source of funds for the share transfer
(e.g. bank account statements, notice of income tax, CPF statements, financial
statements etc.);</p>
<p>(vi) The relationship between the transferor and the transferee;</p>
<p>(vii) Whether the transferee will become a substantial shareholder;</p>
<p>(viii) Whether the transferor’s involvement in the moneylending business
will change;</p>
<p>(ix) A clear copy of the moneylending company’s recent financial statements;</p>
<p>(x) Whether the transferee has any prior experience in moneylending or
debt collecting or has ever applied for a moneylending licence (if yes,
state the business name, period of involvement, designation, job details
etc.); and</p>
<p>(xi) Whether the transferee is related or have any connections with any
persons (e.g. acquaintances/friends/family members/associates etc.) who
has ever been involved in the business of moneylending or debt collecting
or has ever applied for a moneylending licence (if yes, state the person’s
name, the business name, relationship etc.)</p>
<p>7.4 Do note that for all the above updates, the licensee must first
<a href="https://www.gobusiness.gov.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">submit</a>the online application and obtain approval from the Registry
before it could proceed with the update/changes. After the licensee has
received the approval from the Registry, it may then update this information
with ACRA.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>16. How do I renew my moneylending licence?</strong>
<br>The renewal of the moneylending licence is subject to the good conduct
of the licensee. Licensees should have the following documents ready and
forward them to the Registry upon notification, no later than one month
before the expiry of the licence:</p>
<p>(a) A tenancy agreement for the proposed place of business covering at
least a one-year period immediately following the expiry of the existing
licence;</p>
<p>(b) A copy of the proof of ownership of the premises in the form of property
tax or strata title deed;</p>
<p>(c) A landlord’s approval and the Management Corporation Strata Title
(MCST)’s approval (if applicable) for the proposed place of business covering
the entire licence period;</p>
<p>(d) A renewed Banker’s Guarantee or Insurance Guarantee for a sum of $20,000,
covering the entire licence period; and</p>
<p>(e) A duly completed GIRO application form (for licensees who do not have
an existing GIRO arrangement with the Registry for the deduction of $1,500
as the licence fee)</p>
<p>The application for the renewal of the moneylender’s licence will complete
at least 2 weeks before the expiry of the existing licence, subject to
the submission of requisite information/documents and the payment of the
licence fee.</p>
<p>The renewal for approved additional place(s) of business will be processed
concurrently with the renewal of moneylender’s licence. The additional
licence fee payable for the renewal of approved additional place(s) of
business will be $1,500 per annum for every additional place of business.</p>
<p>Do note that the approval of all additional place(s) of business (including
renewed approvals) will terminate upon the expiry or revocation of a licence.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>17. How do I terminate my moneylending licence?</strong>
<br>You may submit your application <a href="https://www.gobusiness.gov.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">here</a> to terminate your moneylending
licence.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>18. How do I apply for a certificate of exemption from the Moneylenders Act?</strong>
<br>Any person may apply under section 91(1) of the Moneylenders Act to exempt
his business from all or any of the provisions of the same Act. You may
submit an application to the Registry via <a href="https://www.gobusiness.gov.sg/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>GoBusiness</u></a> with the following
information ready:</p>
<p>(a) A write-up containing:</p>
<p>(i) a brief description of the applicant’s current business activities;</p>
<p>(ii) a profile of the applicant, including any experience in moneylending
or related business activities; and</p>
<p>(iii) the reason(s) for seeking an exemption</p>
<p>(b) A copy of the applicant’s Business Profile issued by ACRA;</p>
<p>(c) The telephone number(s) of the applicant;</p>
<p>(d) A detailed description of each type of activity for which the exemption
is sought;</p>
<p>(e) Profile(s) of borrowers;</p>
<p>(f) The interest and fees, if any, which the applicant intends to charge;
and</p>
<p>(g) A draft scope of exemption for our consideration
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>19. What are the fees I have to pay for a certification of exemption? What is the validity period of the exemption?</strong>
<br>Depending on the business activity exempted, the fees may range from $1,000
to $4,000 per year. The exemption is valid for a period up to a maximum
of 3 years.</p>

<p><strong>20. How do I extend the validity period of my certificate of exemption?</strong>
<br>To extend your validity period of the certificate of exemption, you are
required to <a href="https://www.gobusiness.gov.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">submit</a> an
application to the Registry at least one month before the expiry of your
existing certificate of exemption.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>21. How do I know if my business qualifies as an excluded moneylender under the Moneylenders Act?</strong>
<br>You may wish to refer to section 2 of the Moneylenders Act, which sets
out the definition of an excluded moneylender. You may wish to seek legal
advice on whether your business activity falls within the definition of
an excluded moneylender.
<br>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>22. How do I make payment when I receive the Notice of Offer of Composition?</strong>
<br>Payment for the Notice of Offer of Composition can be made via our <a href="https://rom.mlaw.gov.sg/composition-and-penalties-payments/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">website</a> portal.</p>
